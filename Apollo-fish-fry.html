Apollo Fish fry

If you are looking for a spicy and alluring non-veg appetizer, go for this restaurant-style hyderabadi special. The batter-fried fillets that are light and crispy on the outside, super moist and flaky on the inside harmonized with a spicy sauce with a bright red hue make it an exotic and inviting starter.

Ingredients

  • Any Boneless Fish fillets (Tilapia was used for this recipe) – 3
  • Chili powder – ½ tsp
  • Kashmiri Red Chili powder – ½ tsp
  • Ginger garlic paste – 1 tsp
  • Turmeric powder – ½ tsp
  • Egg – 1
  • Corn Flour – 2 tsp
  • All-purpose flour – 2 tsp
  • Chili Paste – 4 Kashmiri + 2 normal red chilies
  • Garlic – 2 pods
  • Green chilies – 3
  • Curry leaves – 1 string
  • Curd – 2 tsp
  • Pepper – 1/4 tsp
  • Salt

Directions

Preparation

  1. Soak 4 Kashmiri red chilies along with 2 regular red chilies in hot water for 30 minutes. Drain the chilies and grind it to a smooth thick paste.

  2. Cut the fish pieces into medium-sized rectangles.

  3. In a bowl whisk 1 egg, 1/2 tsp red chili powder, 1/2 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder, 1/2 tsp turmeric powder, 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste, 1/4 tsp pepper powder, and salt. Add in the fish pieces and rub them with the masala mixture.

  4. To this, add 2 tsp of all-purpose flour, 2 tsp of cornflour or rice flour and mix well to coat the fish pieces.

let's get into action

  1. Deep fry these fish fillets in hot oil till the fish is cooked well and pillowy on the inside and the exterior is crisp and golden.

  2. In a wide skillet/pan, heat 2 tsp of oil. Add 2 finely chopped garlic pods, 3 slit green chilies, a string of curry leaves, and sauté them.

  3. Add in the ground chili paste and cook in low to medium flame till the chili paste becomes glossy (takes about 5 minutes).

  4. In low flame, stir in 2 tsp of whisked curd and mix well. Sprinkle some salt and add in the fried fish pieces.

  5. Toss the fish in the sauce mix over high flame for a minute or two. Serve this extra-spicy starter hot!

Recipe tips

  • Lemon juice can be added to the marination paste to make the fish fillets extra tart and tangy.
  • Chili paste shall be prepared just using Kashmiri red chilies to tone down the spice level.
